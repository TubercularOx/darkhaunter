# The Blasphemy Fight

## TubercularOx's Reconstruction

This reconstruction does not attempt to recreate turn or round order.  It focuses on the visible actions of people in the scene.

### Nale
- Turn 1: Nale begins the fight by casting Blasphemy.
- Turn 2+: Nale moves into contact with Roy and grapples him. He appears to be using the option that lets him apply damage with a grapple check, one or more times.

### Haley
- Turn 1: She appears to have a readied action that interrupts Nale's use of blasphemy. It targets Sabine with an arrow.
- Turn 2: She is weakened by the Blasphemy and complains of not being able to draw her bow. This implies her bow is made to allow a Strength bonus to damage, preventing it from being used by a weaker character.

### Roy
- Roy does not get an attack of opportunity when Nale grapples him.  Since he was dazed by Blasphemy, he is probably still flat-footed because he has yet to act in combat. This prevents attacks of opportunity.
- Turn 1: Dazed, no actions.

### Thog
- Turn 1: He moves towards Serini and Serini jumps out of the way. Then he moves by Belkar and takes an attack of opportunity from him. Then he attacks Durkon. Serini ducking out of the way may be an avoided Overrun attack, which wouldn't cost Thog an action.
- Turn 2: He attacks both Durkon and Belkar without moving, implying a full attack action.

### Belkar
- Turn 1: Positions himself between Thog and Durkon, then appears to end his turn without attacking.

### Minrah
- Paralyzed until further notice.

### Sabine
- Turn 1: She moves towards Haley, gets told off, then uses her Greater Teleport to teleport behind Durkon.
- Turn 2: She does a Sneak Attack against Durkon.  The black aura makes sense as her Enery Drain, but Energy Drain normally requires a grapple.

### Durkon
- Turn 1: Dazed, no actions
- Turn 2: Possibly flat-footed when Sabine attacks, letting her initiate a grapple without an attack of opportunity, just like Nale and Roy.
