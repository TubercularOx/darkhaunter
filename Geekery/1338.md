# The Blasphemy Fight

## Tubercular Ox's Reconstruction

This reconstruction does not attempt to recreate initiative or round order. It focuses on the visible actions shown in the comic.

### Nale
- **Turn 1 (1338): Nale begins the fight by casting _Blasphemy_.**
- **Turn 2+ (1339): Nale moves into contact with Roy and grapples him.** He appears to be using the option that allows damage to be dealt with a grapple check one or more times.
- **Turn 3+ (1342): Nale attacks Roy.**
- **(1342): Nale attacks Roy again,** though Roy appears to block this attack.
- **(1342): Nale flings Vaarsuvius into Roy, Elan, and Mr. Scruffy.** There is free-action dialogue around this moment, and Elan also advances, but I'm treating Elan's movement as cosmetic for now.
- **(1343): Nale moves into the next room, talks, and returns to the first room.** This could technically be part of Turn 4.
- **NOTE:** It is possible to interpret Nale's actions as two full attacks with three attacks each. If so, Roy likely does not have Combat Reflexes, since he only strikes back once during the second sequence.

### Haley
- **Turn 1 (1338): Haley appears to have a readied action** that interrupts Nale's use of _Blasphemy_. It targets Sabine with an arrow.
- **Turn 2 (1339): Haley is weakened by _Blasphemy_ and complains that she cannot draw her bow.** This suggests her bow is built to allow a Strength bonus to damage, preventing a weaker character from drawing it.

### Roy
- **(1339): Roy does not receive an attack of opportunity when Nale grapples him.** Since he was dazed by _Blasphemy_ and has not yet acted in combat, he is probably still flat-footed.
- **Turn 1 (1339): Roy is dazed and takes no actions.**
- **Turn 2 (1341): Roy full-attacks Nale.**
- **(1342): Roy attacks Nale.** Since this does not appear to be part of another full attack, it may be an attack of opportunity triggered by Nale's unimproved unarmed strike, though other interpretations are possible.

### Thog
- **Turn 1 (1339): Thog moves toward Serini, who jumps out of the way.** He then moves past Belkar, provoking an attack of opportunity, and attacks Durkon. Serini's movement may represent an avoided overrun attempt, which would not cost Thog an action.
- **Turn 2 (1339): Thog attacks both Durkon and Belkar in a single swing.** Durkon is shown down in (1340), suggesting this attack triggered Cleave.
- **(1340): Thog attacks Belkar.** I place this as part of a full attack because Thog is considerably ahead of most characters in visible actions.
- **Turn 3 (1341): Thog attacks Durkon.** Elan's spellcasting prevents this attack from being folded into the previous full attack sequence.
- **Turn 4 (1345): Thog attacks Belkar.**
- **(1345): Thog attacks Sabine.** This is likely an attack of opportunity triggered when Sabine attempts to move away.

### Belkar
- **Turn 1 (1339): Belkar positions himself between Thog and Durkon, then appears to end his turn without attacking.**
- **(1340): Belkar blocks Thog's attack** with a two-weapon cross-block and a "Block!" sound effect. This could represent Two-Weapon Defense granting a shield bonus while wielding two weapons.
- **Turn 2 (1341): Belkar attacks Thog.**
- **Turn 3 (1342): Belkar jumps at Thog.**

### Minrah
- **(1339): Minrah is paralyzed until further notice.**

### Sabine
- **Turn 1 (1339): Sabine moves toward Haley, receives a warning, then uses _Greater Teleport_ to appear behind Durkon.**
- **Turn 2 (1339): Sabine sneak attacks Durkon.** The black aura makes sense as her Energy Drain, but Energy Drain normally requires a grapple. My memory is that there are other instances of Sabine attacking this way, so it may be a design choice by Rich.
- **Turn 3 (1345): Sabine attacks Lien.**
- **(1345): Sabine attempts to move toward O-Chul and Sunny.** This is probably part of the same turn.

### Durkon
- **Turn 1 (1339): Durkon is dazed and takes no actions.**
- **(1340): Durkon appears to be down.** This is consistent with Thog's previous swing having triggered Cleave.

### Lien
- **Turn 1: Recovery from Dazed is implied.**
- **Turn 2 (1340): Lien attacks Sabine, who dodges with a "Dodge!" sound effect.** Lien appears weakened, and the black spiral may indicate the dazed effect ending.
- **Turn 3 (1341): Lien attacks Sabine and misses.**
- **Turn 4 (1345): Lien attacks Sabine and misses.** Could be two swings and two misses depending on how you interpret the art.

### Vaarsuvius
- **Turn 1 (1339): Recovery from Dazed is implied.**
- **Turn 2 (1340): Vaarsuvius attempts _Sound Lance_** and is immediately placed into time-out by the IFCC.

### Sunny
- **Turn 1: Recovery from Dazed is implied.**
- **Turn 2 (1341): Sunny fires several eye rays.**
- **Turn 3 (1345): Sunny fires _Charm Monster_ at Nale.**
- **Turn 4 (1345): Sunny fires _Charm Monster_ at Thog.** The art strongly suggests O-Chul is directing the same eye twice.

### O-Chul
- **Turn 1: Recovery from Dazed is implied.**
- **Turn 2 (1341): O-Chul identifies Sunny's _Charm Monster_ eye, rotates Sunny, and takes aim.**
- **(1345): O-Chul assists Sunny with aiming and firing,** though if he used any actual actions is unclear.
